Cyber Security

What is Cyber Security?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at:

  • Accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information.
  • Extorting money from users (via ransomware).
  • Interrupting normal business processes.

It’s like a digital shield that guards everything from your personal smartphone to a multinational corporation's data centers.

Work in Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity involves a wide range of roles and responsibilities, including:

1. Network Security

  • Protecting the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of networks.

2. Information Security

  • Safeguarding data from unauthorized access and alteration.

3. Application Security

  • Ensuring that software is secure from threats.

4. Operational Security

  • Procedures and decisions for handling and protecting data assets.

Importance of Cybersecurity

Why is it important?

  • Data Protection: To secure personal, financial, and confidential information.
  • National Security: Governments need cybersecurity to protect classified and critical infrastructure.
  • Business Reputation: Companies risk massive loss of trust and revenue after a breach.
  • Compliance: Many industries have strict regulations (like GDPR, HIPAA, etc.).
  • Ransomware & Cyber Threats: Increasing threats make cybersecurity a must-have, not a luxury.

Scope of Cybersecurity

Explosive Growth in Demand

  • Cyber threats are growing every single day.
  • According to various global reports, there will be 3.5 million unfilled cybersecurity jobs globally by the end of 2025.
  • In India alone, there’s a shortage of over 2 lakh cybersecurity professionals.
  • Every industry is going digital, which means every industry needs cybersecurity — not just IT.
